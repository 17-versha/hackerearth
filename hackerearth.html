REVOLUTION IN TRANSPORT SYSTEM

A new approach

Take A Tour

ISSUE

EVERINCREASING population and automobiles AND exponentially increasing TRAFFIC and ENVIRONMENT-THREATS.

OUR OBJECTIVE

To build Fast and efficient transport system which uses small technically equipped oval shaped compartments to carry people safely with high speed on tracks laid on roads which will help reducing pollution and ensure faster travelling.

SAVE FUEL

Reduced waiting hours ensures sustainable usage of fuels.

REDUCE NOISE POLLUTION

Honking in hilly areas causes landslides and disturbs wildlife.

EFFECTIVE USE OF LAND

The track system removes the extra space occupied by big vehicles and their vacant seats. It will also remove the illegal accession of land by road-side vendors.

SMALL HUMAN-CARRYING VEHICLES EQUIPPED WITH GPS

WELL LAID TRACKS AND SMALL OVERBRIDGES AT CROSS-STREETS

“A 2 way track for narrow roads for vehicles to travel in opposite direction and a 4 way track for broader roads ,with two adjacent tracks allowing movement in one direction and the other two in opposite direction,WITH proper track-switch.”

PROJECT-OUTLINE

Outlook

Two tracks can be laid on the road , one for forward movement and other for backward movement. At every 1 km a low maintainance small station can be created for the compartments to load and unload passengers. A passenger can attach as many number of compartments required. Both sides of the track contain a broad margin for pedestreans,cycles and other vehicles etc. These compartments are self driven with a constant speed and APPROXIMATELY 3.5 feet in diameter. The compartments are made to travel approx 100 meters at the desired acceleration approximately 0.72 m/second square) before joining the main track to catch up with the speed of already moving compartments running with an approximate constant speed of 40 kmph.

A SENSOR is placed at a distance of 282.84 metres from the end where the compartment joins the main track. If the SENSOR is found to be without pressure at any instance and the space is equal to 3 times the diameter of the selected number of compartments a signal is sent and the first set of compartments is prepared to join the track at the opening. The method not only ensures that people walk but also ensures less emmission of greenhouse gases. When any person reaches the destination point, the compartment detaches from the main track and moves to an auxilliary track in the same way as during joining and takes the compartment to the booth with decreasing acceleration and ultimately stoping it at desired booth. The complete setup is also expected to boost employment and reduce cases of bullying and physical harrasments caused in public tranports.
